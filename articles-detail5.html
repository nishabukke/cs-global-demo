SPEND ANALYSIS

What is Spend Analysis?

Spend Analysis is a strategic process used by procurement and finance teams to review and analyze an organization’s expenditure. It helps businesses make informed decisions, reduce costs, and optimize supplier relationships. Here's a breakdown of what Spend Analysis entails:

Key Elements of Spend Analysis

1. Who’s Purchasing?

Identifying the departments or cost centers responsible for purchases.

2. What Are We Purchasing?

Analyzing the goods and services being procured.

3. How Much Have We Paid?

Reviewing the total amount spent on procurement activities.

4. How Much Have We Purchased?

Measuring the volume or quantity of goods/services bought.

5. When Was It Purchased?

Tracking the time of purchases to spot trends and opportunities for savings.

6. Who Are We Purchasing From?

Analyzing the suppliers providing the goods and services.

7. How Much Did It Cost?

Evaluating the prices paid for better cost control and supplier negotiations.

By answering these critical questions, Spend Analysis enables businesses to improve procurement efficiency, identify cost-saving opportunities, and foster more strategic supplier partnerships.
